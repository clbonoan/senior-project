Information

This project aims to train a machine learning model to detect image tampering through shadow-based features such as texture, lighting, and depth. The focus is on urban infrastructure, where aerial and ground-level images are frequently utilized to monitor potential projects and development progress. We plan to develop a web application that analyzes an image's shadows and provides a tampering likelihood score with a visual of suspicious regions. Our approach uses pixel-based algorithms to extract shadow texture, lighting strength, and depth characteristics to improve detection accuracy. We seek to support digital forensics and ensure the authenticity of urban imagery used in planning and reporting.
